About Our Mission

The Blues Heritage Project was founded in 2015 with a singular vision: to preserve, celebrate, and share the transformative legacy of John Lee Hooker and the broader blues tradition that shaped American culture. Our organization emerged from the recognition that critical archival materials were at risk of being lost to time, and that the profound contributions of blues pioneers deserved comprehensive documentation and scholarly attention. What began as a small initiative among passionate historians and musicians has evolved into a thriving global community dedicated to ensuring that future generations can access, study, and appreciate this vital cultural heritage.

At our core, we believe that music is a universal language that transcends boundaries and generations. Our mission is to democratize access to high-quality educational content, foster meaningful connections among enthusiasts worldwide, and actively support preservation efforts that protect irreplaceable recordings and historical artifacts. We are committed to maintaining rigorous academic standards while remaining accessible to casual listeners, creating an inclusive space where scholars, musicians, students, and fans can collaborate, learn, and celebrate the enduring spirit of John Lee Hooker's music and its continued influence on contemporary artists and cultural movements.
